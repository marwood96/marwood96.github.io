Mental Health: Why it matters

Mental Health is a rather broad, sometimes vague term, it is a complex collective of thoughts, emotions and behaviours; it is an extremely important part of our bodies overall wellness and has a massive effect on a person's quality of life. Ensuring a healthy mind means taking care of and addressing our feelings, seeking assistance when it is needed and recognising when things unhealthy habits and relationships need to change.

Due to there being no obvious visible symptoms of mental health illnesses, many shrug off those afflicted by mental illnesses as being 'soft-skinned' or seeking attention, this can lead to people feeling afraid to seek help and to openly speak on the subject. More and more it is becoming essential to promote mental health awareness and remove the stigma surrounding it in order to open up new avenues of help and treatment for the conditions.

Looking to start your exercise journey?

Getting started with a fitness programme can feel daunting and overwhelming, if you are looking for helpful advice on how to begin your exercise journey, click below for helpful tips and advice.

Join a club!

Joining an exercise club can be beneficial in providing motivation towards keeping up with physical exercise, aswell as allowing you to meet new people it can also offer guidance into how to correctly and safely perform the activities.

Low energy activities

Upon first starting your exercise journey, it can be overwhelming
and tough to complete certain exercises. Fortunately there are plenty of activities that can be performed by people of any ability;
yoga, walking and cycling are all excellent starters!

Get involved in sports

A common reason people are hesitant to exercise is due to it being boring or lonely. Joining a sports club, whether it be football, rugby, tennis, or whatever you fancy; can offer the chance to meet new people, and have fun whilst you get healthier!

Find a gym

Joining a gym can offer many benefits for your health journey,
the gym can be a distraction from stress, offers a place where there are other like-minded people who also want to be healther, and can include trainers who are able to guide you in using equipment and getting started!

More advice

Talk to someone

Experts recommend talking to friends, family or healthcare professionals about feelings of depression or anxiety, as they can be valuble pillars of support.

Find a new hobby

Finding a new hobby, whether it's something to do at home, or joining a new club, can help you meet new people and provide a healthy distraction.

Nutrition

Ensuring a balanced, healthy diet can have a significantly positive effect on the health of your brain.
